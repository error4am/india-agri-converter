Frequently Asked Questions About Bigha to Square Meter Conversion

How many square meters is 1 bigha?

1 bigha equals 1,618.74 square meters in Uttar Pradesh, 5,043.2 in Punjab, 2,023.45 in Rajasthan, and 1,428.4 in West Bengal. The variation is due to different historical standardization in each state. Always select your state for accurate measurements.

Why is square meter important?

Square meter (sq m) is the international SI unit for area measurement. It's used by architects, engineers, surveyors, and government agencies worldwide. Converting to square meters ensures: universal understanding, compatibility with international standards, scientific precision, and acceptance by banks and government bodies.

Is square meter more accurate than bigha?

Square meter and bigha can both be equally accurate. However, square meter has the advantage of being a globally standardized unit defined by the International System of Units (SI). Bigha varies by state, so measurements in square meters are easier to compare across regions and internationally.

Can I use this for legal property documents?

Yes, this converter provides official government-recognized conversions. However, for legal property documents, bank loans, government registration, or any official transaction, always get measurements certified by a licensed land surveyor. This tool is perfect for quick calculations and verification.

Why do different states have different bigha sizes?

During British colonial rule and after independence, different Indian states standardized land measurements based on local historical practices and administrative needs. These regional standards became official and persist today. Modern surveyors use these state-specific bigha values for all official measurements and documentation.
