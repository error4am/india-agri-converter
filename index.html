Land Measurement Units in India

India uses various traditional land measurement units alongside international standards. Different states standardized measurements differently, creating regional variations that persist today. Understanding these units is essential for farmers, real estate professionals, government workers, and property buyers across India.

Common Indian Land Units

  • Acre: International standard = 4,046.86 square meters
  • Hectare: Metric standard = 10,000 square meters
  • Bigha: Traditional unit varying by state (1,428 to 6,071 sq m)
  • Kanal: Used in Punjab & Haryana = 1,012.85 square meters
  • Marla: Quarter of kanal = 25.29 square meters
  • Guntha: Used in South India = 101 square meters
  • Cent: Used in South India = 40.4 square meters
  • Biswa: Traditional unit = 2,023.45 square meters
  • Square Feet: Imperial unit = 0.092903 square meters
  • Square Meter: SI unit = 1 square meter

Each region of India has its own preferred measurements, making a universal converter essential. Our tool accounts for all these variations and helps you understand the differences.
